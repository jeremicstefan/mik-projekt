# Company History — English Content
<!-- Source text for translation. Serbian version lives in o-nama.html -->

## 1931 — The Roots: Rapid Zagreb
The story begins with a family. Ivan Simeoni founds Rapid — a boiler manufacturing company based in Zagreb — establishing the first industrial boiler-building technology in Croatia. Over the next 15 years, the Simeoni family builds Rapid into a center of engineering excellence, training a generation of specialist boiler engineers who would go on to shape the entire industry.

## 1946 — TPK Zagreb Is Born
Following nationalization process in Yugoslavia, Rapid is unified with two other Zagreb-based companies — Progres and Tespa — to form Tvornica Parnih Kotlova Zagreb (TPK). The newly formed company, which includes a dedicated Project and Engineering Bureau based in Belgrade, starts with 133 employees and immediately establishes itself as Yugoslavia's leading boiler manufacturing enterprise. By 1948, following a move to a new facility in Žitnjak, the workforce grows to 213.

## 1960 — Three Decades of Projects: A Track Record Takes Shape
By 1960, the Belgrade Engineering Bureau has been operating for 14 years, delivering boiler house projects across Yugoslavia. This milestone marks the point at which the bureau's accumulated experience and project volume firmly establish it as the leading force in industrial heating infrastructure in the region — laying the foundation for everything that follows.

## 1970–1980 — Fuel Transition: From Solid to Liquid
A decade of intensive technological evolution. TPK and its Engineering Bureau lead the conversion of industrial heating systems from solid fuel to liquid fuel, positioning the organization at the forefront of combustion engineering in the region.

## 1974 — Đorđe Delikladić Becomes CEO of TPK
Having built his career at TPK as a mechanical engineer, Đorđe Delikladić is appointed CEO of Tvornica Parnih Kotlova Zagreb. Under his leadership, TPK reaches its peak scale — employing 2,389 people by 1980 — and cements its position as the dominant force in industrial boiler manufacturing in Yugoslavia.

## 1990 — The Gas Era Begins
In response to shifting energy markets and the rapid expansion of natural gas infrastructure, the Engineering Bureau pioneers the conversion of boiler systems to gaseous fuel — a transition that defines the next generation of industrial heating.

## 1991 — MIK Projekt Is Founded
As Yugoslavia dissolves, Đorđe Delikladić separates the Belgrade Engineering Bureau from TPK Zagreb and establishes it as an independent company — MIK Projekt d.o.o., headquartered in Belgrade. Decades of accumulated expertise, relationships, and technical knowledge move forward under a new name, with full continuity of people and purpose.

## 2016 — NOx Reduction: Engineering for Cleaner Combustion
MIK Projekt develops and implements its first dedicated NOx reduction projects, responding to tightening European environmental standards and growing client demand for low-emission combustion solutions.

## 2019 — Production Facility Established in Nočaj
MIK Projekt opens its own manufacturing facility in Nočaj, Serbia — completing the vertical integration of the business from engineering and design through to in-house component production and assembly.

## 2022 — Ready for the Energy Transition
MIK Projekt expands its focus to next-generation energy solutions: hydrogen-ready systems, industrial heat pumps, and electric boilers. Under the leadership of Igor and Aleksandar Delikladić — sons of the founder — the company positions itself as a partner for clients navigating the shift toward decarbonized industrial heat, carrying three generations of engineering knowledge into the future.
